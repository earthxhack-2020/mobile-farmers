What is a food dessert?

A food desert is an area that has limited access to affordable and nutritious food. In 2010, the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) reported that 23.5 million Americans live in “food deserts”, meaning that they live more than one mile from a supermarket in urban or suburban areas and more than 10 miles from a supermarket in rural areas.


The difference between a rural and an urban food desert is the population density of residents and their distance from the nearest supermarket. Twenty percent of rural areas in the U.S. are classified as food deserts.[31] There are small areas within each state in the U.S. that are classified as rural food deserts, but they occur most prominently in the Midwest[32] Within these counties, approximately 2.4 million individuals have low access to a large supermarket

How is Mobile Farmer’s Market helping residents who live in food deserts?

We eliminate the need for residents to travel long distances to get fresh food. We keep them updated every time a farmer is doing a drop-off near their location by providing SMS updates.

How is Mobile Farmer’s Market helping farmers?

We’re helping farmers stay connected with people who need to purchase their products. They are able to decide when and where they want to sale, and also have updates directly sent to customers.
